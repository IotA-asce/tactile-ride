# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

TactileRide contributors and maintainers commit to a welcoming, respectful,
evidence-oriented community. We value curiosity, constructive disagreement, and
care around claims that could affect physical prototypes or rider behaviour.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful, specific, and open to correction.
- Challenge ideas, test methods, and claims without attacking people.
- State assumptions, limitations, and uncertainty plainly.
- Respect privacy and avoid sharing personal information without permission.
- Prioritise safety and responsible development over urgency or novelty.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, discriminatory language, threats, or personal attacks.
- Deliberately misleading safety, test, compatibility, or certification claims.
- Sharing another person's private information without permission.
- Disruptive behaviour that prevents good-faith collaboration.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report concerns privately to a project maintainer using a contact method the
repository owner has published. Do not post sensitive personal information in a
public issue. Maintainers should acknowledge reports, gather only necessary
context, decide proportionate action, and protect confidentiality where possible.
